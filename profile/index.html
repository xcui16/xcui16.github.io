New York Statewide COVID-19 Testing From 2020 to 2024

COVID-19 is an infectious disease, initially identified in 2019. It has been declared as a global pandemic by the World Health Organization (WHO) in March 2020. It has spread worldwide quickly and had a profound impact on global health, economies, education and social life, including the New York State, the United States. Although it was officially over, this COVID-19 pandemic has led to 80k-plus New Yorker’s deaths.

New York State is the fourth-most populous state in the United States. It is divided into ten regions, including the Capital Region, Central New York, Finger Lakes, Long Island, Mid-Hudson, Mohawk Valley, New York City, North, Country, Southern Tier, and Western New York.

However, the population of New York State is inappropriately distributed across different regions. The population are concentrated in the southeast and west of this state, particularly in New York City. Although this city has the smallest land area in the state, New York City has the greatest population with 8,804,190.

Since it occurred, hospitals have been overwhelmed. More seriously, thousands of health workers got infected and hospitals were lack of medical resources. The outbreak of COVID-19 in New York State raised the attention of related institutions. The New York State Department of Health has recorded new positive growth in this state by county, as well as the region since March 1, 2020. From 2020 to 2024, the number of people infected with the COVID-19 virus in New York City has reached nearly half of the total number of new cases in the state.

From the perspective of the infection rate, the infection situation of COVID-19 has a close relationship with population density. Areas with higher population density are more likely to experience faster and more widespread transmission of the virus. Overall, the pattern of infection across different regions shows a similar trend.
